COS568 Learned Index Project
Task 1 Summary

All Task 1 experiments were run from the scratch workspace to avoid space limits in the repo checkout:
/scratch/network/ef0952/COS568-LI-SP26-task1

Method
- Downloaded the Facebook, Books, and OSMC 100M uint64 datasets.
- Generated the four Task 1 workloads for each dataset.
- Built the benchmark binary.
- Ran the full sweep of 3 datasets x 3 indexes x 4 workloads with 3 repeats each.
- Selected, for each index and workload, the row with the highest average throughput across the 3 runs.

Best throughput results (Mops/s)

Dataset: FB
- LIPP: lookup-only 358.300, insert throughput (50/50) 1.378, lookup after insert 349.263, mixed 10% insert 13.488, mixed 90% insert 2.158
- BTree: lookup-only 1.694, insert throughput (50/50) 1.059, lookup after insert 1.546, mixed 10% insert 1.470, mixed 90% insert 1.082
- DynamicPGM: lookup-only 1.797, insert throughput (50/50) 6.192, lookup after insert 0.471, mixed 10% insert 0.849, mixed 90% insert 2.799

Dataset: Books
- LIPP: lookup-only 361.350, insert throughput (50/50) 2.291, lookup after insert 358.773, mixed 10% insert 17.526, mixed 90% insert 2.855
- BTree: lookup-only 1.690, insert throughput (50/50) 1.084, lookup after insert 1.572, mixed 10% insert 1.477, mixed 90% insert 1.058
- DynamicPGM: lookup-only 1.947, insert throughput (50/50) 6.124, lookup after insert 0.530, mixed 10% insert 0.935, mixed 90% insert 2.972

Dataset: OSMC
- LIPP: lookup-only 307.095, insert throughput (50/50) 0.933, lookup after insert 253.168, mixed 10% insert 10.447, mixed 90% insert 1.621
- BTree: lookup-only 2.063, insert throughput (50/50) 1.032, lookup after insert 1.826, mixed 10% insert 1.641, mixed 90% insert 1.046
- DynamicPGM: lookup-only 1.804, insert throughput (50/50) 5.332, lookup after insert 0.455, mixed 10% insert 0.948, mixed 90% insert 2.663

Overall averages across the three datasets
- Lookup-only: LIPP 342.248, DynamicPGM 1.849, BTree 1.816
- Insert throughput (50/50): DynamicPGM 5.883, LIPP 1.534, BTree 1.058
- Lookup after insert (50/50): LIPP 320.401, BTree 1.648, DynamicPGM 0.485
- Mixed 10% insert: LIPP 13.820, BTree 1.529, DynamicPGM 0.911
- Mixed 90% insert: DynamicPGM 2.811, LIPP 2.211, BTree 1.062

Interpretation
- LIPP is the clear winner for read-heavy workloads. Its learned model predicts exact positions, so it avoids the extra corrective search that the other structures still pay.
- DynamicPGM is the clear winner for insertion-heavy workloads. Its amortized, buffered update strategy gives much higher insert throughput than the other two indexes.
- BTree is the middle ground. It is consistently more balanced than the extremes, but it does not win the pure lookup or pure insertion cases in these runs.
- In practice from these measurements: use LIPP when lookups dominate, use DynamicPGM when inserts dominate, and treat BTree as the stable baseline.

Notes on hyperparameters
- BTree usually performed best with LinearSearch and value 8.
- DynamicPGM lookup-only usually preferred small values such as 16.
- DynamicPGM insertion-heavy workloads usually preferred larger values such as 64, 128, 256, 512, or 1024 depending on workload and dataset.
